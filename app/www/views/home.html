Benefits

Medical misdiagnoses are an all-too-common feature of healthcare – as many as 12 million Americans are misdiagnosed by their physicians every year. These misdiagnoses not only lead to increased costs for patients but present serious medical risks in as much as 50 percent of such cases.

Various medical research indicates second opinions provide significant value to patients. Not only can they increase the accuracy of diagnoses, or prevent unnecessary treatments, but they also provide patients with added comfort when faced with difficult medical decisions. And at a time when most individuals and households are paying sizable insurance co-payments and deductibles, a reliable second opinion can help them save hundreds or even thousands of dollars.

But seeking a second opinion can often be a challenge. Where should patients or their families turn? How far might they have to travel? If their doctor recommends another doctor, will they simply repeat the initial diagnosis?
